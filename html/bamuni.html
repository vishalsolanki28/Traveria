Bamuni hills tezpur

Tezpur, a beautiful city on the south bank of the mighty Brahmaputra ( Son of Brahma- the only male river in India); is dotted with verdant hills, canopies of Amaltas ( Cassia Fistula), Gulmohar and Palash trees extend far onto the horizon. The reflection of a winter Sun creates myriads of dancing luminous suns on the placid river, a solitary wooden boat rocks gently tethered to a bamboo pole, a few water hyacinth gently flows by, the cool breeze carries the smell of the river far inland. A feeling of timelessness and a sense of eternal peace pervade your soul. Replete with stories from the Mahabharata days, the fabled Agnigarh Hill remind one of the romance between Usha and Anirudh and the celestial war between Lord Krishna and Lord Shiva turning the mighty river red. Dah Parvatiya, a small village on the west side of the town boast of an ancient fifth century Shiva temple suggesting an antiquated advanced culture prevailing on this enchanting land.
